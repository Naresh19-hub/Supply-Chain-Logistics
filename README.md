# Supply-Chain-Logistics

## 🚨 What if improving logistics performance isn’t about one big change… but many small ones—and even missing data?

I worked on a logistics dataset expecting to find strong patterns.

But something unexpected happened 👇

---

## 📊 Most relationships were surprisingly weak:

* Traffic vs Delay → negligible
* Weather vs Fatigue → almost flat
* Time vs Delay → no clear trend

---

## 📉 Additional Observation:

Most variables showed **very low variation**, with values remaining almost constant across categories.

> This suggests the dataset may reflect **controlled or simulated patterns**, where real-world variability is limited.

---

At this point, it felt like the data wasn’t telling anything meaningful.

---

## 🔥 Then I found one strong signal:

✔ **Risk Classification ↔ Disruption Likelihood (R² ≈ 0.99)**

This clearly showed:

> Higher risk → higher disruptions

---

But something still didn’t feel complete.

---

## 🧠 That’s when the real insight came:

> Maybe the problem isn’t just weak data… maybe something important is missing.

---

 🚚 Missing High-Impact Factors (Real-World Thinking):

* Last-mile accessibility
* Customer availability
* Route quality & road conditions
* Warehouse location efficiency
* Order density / delivery clustering

👉 These factors often have **more real-world impact than the variables in the dataset**

---

## 📈 Business Perspective:

To improve performance from **90% → 92%**:

* You can’t rely on one factor
* You must combine multiple small improvements
* AND consider missing real-world drivers

---

## ⚙️ My Approach:

* Performed EDA using Excel (Pivot Tables, Charts)
* Evaluated relationships using R²
* Identified strong vs weak signals
* Extended analysis beyond dataset using domain thinking

---

## 💡 Final Thought:

> Data analysis is not just about finding patterns—
> it’s about understanding what matters, what doesn’t… and what’s missing.
